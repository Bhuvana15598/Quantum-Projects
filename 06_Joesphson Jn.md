A simple simulation illustrating the fundamental physics of a Josephson junction, the core building block of superconducting quantum computers.

Overview:

This project demonstrates:

1. Current–phase relationship of a Josephson junction
2. Josephson energy landscape as a function of phase
3. Formation of discrete quantum energy levels
4. Anharmonicity that enables selective qubit control
5. The physical basis of transmon qubits

Key Concepts:


1. Josephson Effect: Supercurrent flows through a junction without voltage.
2. Current–Phase Relation: Current depends sinusoidally on the phase difference across the junction.
3. Anharmonic Energy Levels: Uneven energy spacing allows the |0⟩ and |1⟩ states to function as a qubit.
4. Transmon Qubits: Widely used in superconducting quantum processors.

Learning Objective:

Understand how Josephson junctions create quantized energy levels and form the foundation of superconducting qubit architectures used in modern quantum computing.

Applications

Used in quantum processors developed by companies such as IBM and Google.
