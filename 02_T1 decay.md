A simple simulation illustrating T1 relaxation, the process by which a qubit loses its excited-state population over time due to energy dissipation.

Overview:

This project demonstrates:

1. Exponential decay of qubit excited-state population
2. Comparison of qubit lifetimes for different material qualities
3. Visualization of T1 relaxation curves
4. Identification of the 1/e decay point, which defines the T1 time constant
5. Basic verification of the analytical solution using density matrix concepts

Output:



The program:

1. Generates a plot showing T1 decay for multiple material-quality scenarios
2. Highlights the T1 lifetime for each curve
3. Saves the visualization as T1_decay.png
4. Prints key physical interpretations and verification results

Physics Insight:

1. T1 represents the characteristic time for a qubit to relax from its excited state toward its ground state.
2. At t = T1, the excited-state population decreases to 1/e ≈ 36.8% of its initial value.
3. Longer T1 times indicate better qubit coherence and higher material quality.

Learning Objective:

Understand the relationship between material quality, qubit lifetime, and quantum coherence through a simple analytical model of T1 relaxation.
