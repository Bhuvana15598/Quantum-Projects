A simulation and visualization project based on experimental results from a KTH Royal Institute of Technology M.Sc. thesis, modeling how High Water Vapor Annealing (HWA) improves silicon quantum dot (Si QD) optical stability.

Overview:

This project demonstrates:

1. Quantum dot ON/OFF blinking behavior using a two-level system model
2. Effect of HWA treatment on quantum dot stability
3. Comparison of ON-state population decay before and after HWA
4. Analysis of duty cycle improvement and blinking frequency reduction


Output:



The program generates:

1. ON-state population decay curves
2. Duty cycle comparison charts
3. Blinking frequency comparison plots

Key Findings:

1. HWA treatment increases duty cycle by approximately 10–12%
2. ON/OFF blinking frequency decreases by approximately 0.04 Hz
3. Surface passivation improves quantum dot stability by reducing non-radiative recombination pathways


Learning Objective:

Explore how surface engineering techniques improve silicon quantum dot performance and understand their relevance to quantum information and semiconductor qubit technologies.
