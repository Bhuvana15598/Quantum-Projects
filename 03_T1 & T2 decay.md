A simple quantum computing simulation that visualizes the difference between energy relaxation (T1) and coherence decay (T2) in a qubit.

Overview:

This project demonstrates:

1. T1 Relaxation – loss of excited-state population over time
2. T2 Relaxation – loss of quantum phase coherence
3. Exponential decay behavior of both processes
4. Comparison of qubit lifetime and coherence lifetime
5. Visualization of the characteristic 1/e decay point
   
Output:


The program:

1. Plots T1 and T2 decay curves on the same graph
2. Marks the corresponding T1 and T2 time constants
3. Prints a brief physical interpretation of the results
   
Key Concepts:

1. T1: Measures how quickly a qubit loses energy.
2. T2: Measures how quickly a qubit loses phase information.
3. A qubit may still retain energy while already losing coherence.
4. Quantum computation performance is often limited by T2.

Learning Objective

Understand the fundamental distinction between qubit energy relaxation and coherence loss, and why both are critical for building reliable quantum computers.
